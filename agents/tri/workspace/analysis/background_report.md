# Background Report: Tri Dao

## Biography Synthesis

### Early Education & Foundations
Tri Dao completed his Ph.D. at Stanford University's Department of Computer Science—one of the world's top programs for computer science research. Stanford has a strong tradition in both machine learning and systems research.

### Ph.D. Research & Mentorship
- **Institution**: Stanford University
- **Department**: Computer Science
- **Research Focus**: Machine learning systems, particularly efficient attention and sequence models
- **Key Collaborators**: Christopher Ré, Albert Gu, Stefano Ermon

### Career Progression
1. **Assistant Professor at Princeton University**: Leading research in ML systems at a top-tier institution.

2. **Co-founder & Chief Scientist at Together AI**: Building a company focused on efficient AI infrastructure and open-source models.

3. **Ph.D. at Stanford**: Developed groundbreaking work on FlashAttention and state space models.

### Research Evolution
Tri's research has rapidly evolved from:
- **Attention optimization** (FlashAttention series) → 
- **Structured matrices** (Monarch) → 
- **State space models** (Mamba, in collaboration with Albert Gu)

This progression shows a focus on fundamental efficiency improvements that enable new capabilities.

## Summary
Tri Dao is a rising star in machine learning systems research, having created some of the most impactful innovations in recent years. FlashAttention revolutionized how we train and serve transformers, and Mamba (with Albert Gu) offers a compelling alternative architecture. As both a professor at Princeton and Chief Scientist at Together AI, he bridges academic research with industrial impact. His work consistently demonstrates how algorithmic innovation combined with hardware awareness can lead to dramatic efficiency improvements.
